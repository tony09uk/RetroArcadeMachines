Why this project started

Retro gaming holds a specical place in my heart. Stood at my game of choice surrounded by the noise and flashing lights of other machines, combined with the smell of some sugary snacks hanging in the air brings back fond memories of my time at the local seaside arcades.

Everytime I take the kids to the seaside I find myself keeping an eye out for games I used to play while growing up. Something that I would sink all my pocket money in to and from time to time I find them sat there in some corner, at the back of the arcade, neglected and overlooked. I drop in some pennies and re-live my teenage years.

I know that I am not the only person who loves the old games and that many use emulators to play at home. But to get that real arcade feeling there is nothing like playing in the arcade itself, so I decided to create a place where people could share the locations of these little boxes of history. This project will be nothing without a community backing it so I'm really hoping that others are set and ready to help out.

If you want to help out, you can do so by adding a gaming location or you can support this project through adding features or helping with testing, styling or simply offering a suggestion to improve the site
